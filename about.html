Chapter 1 - The Boy Who Chose Two Worlds

My name is Dilshad Ansari, although most people know me as Dillu. I was born on 12 August 2008 in Ramgarh, Palamu, Jharkhand. From a young age I believed that dreams should never be limited by the place where a person is born.


Today I am studying at Darul Huda Islamic University (DHIU) in Kerala. Moving away from my hometown gave me a new perspective on life. It helped me grow academically, spiritually, and personally.


I can communicate in English, Hindi, Urdu, and Arabic. Each language has shaped my journey in a different way and opened new opportunities to learn from different cultures and communities.


My father, MD Ayub Ansari, is a government teacher who taught me the importance of education and discipline. My mother, Salda Bibi, is a homemaker whose love, patience, and sacrifices have always supported my dreams.


Our family consists of six members. Living in a close-knit family taught me respect, responsibility, and the importance of helping others.


One of the most unique things about me is that my greatest inspiration is my own work. Every project I complete motivates me to improve myself and learn something new. I believe that action creates inspiration.


Joining Darul Huda Islamic University became one of the biggest turning points of my life. It strengthened my faith while encouraging me to think about how technology can be used for education and the benefit of society.


This is only the first chapter of my story. I hope to continue learning, building meaningful projects, and contributing to both Islamic scholarship and modern technology.

"Some people choose between tradition and technology. I chose to learn both."
